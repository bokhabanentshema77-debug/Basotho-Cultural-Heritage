The brief discription of Basotho music and dances

The Basotho people normally do their traditional dances and music on their special occasions.
The dances are normally done when there are special occasions like when people are getting married there will be some rejoicing after that as a dance.
But with music it is way too different because Basotho men can just sing the mokorotlo song when they are chilling at Khotla ea banna for no reason.
Some of their songs are just for encourage them to have peace.

DANCES

INSTRUMENTS

Mohobelo

It is a traditional Basotho dance played by Basotho men or boys.It is often played on traditional holidays.

Mokhibo

It is a traditional Basotho dance played by Basotho women or girls.It is often played on traditional holidays.

Selia-lia

It is a traditional Basotho dance played by Basotho girls.It is often played on traditional holidays or after a certain event

Sephumula

It is a traditional Basotho dance played by Basotho girls or boys.It is often played on traditional holidays.

Ndlamo

It is a traditional Basotho dance played by Basotho boys.It is often played on traditional holidays.

Liphotha

It is a traditional Basotho dance played by Basotho boys.It is often played on traditional holidays.

tenye

It is a traditional Basotho dance played by Basotho girls or boys.It is often played on traditional holidays.

Litolobonya

It is a traditional Basotho dance played by Basotho women.It is often played on traditional holidays.

Mokorotlo

It is a traditional Basotho dance played by males.It is often played on important traditional occasions.

Mokopu

It is a traditional Basotho dance played by Basotho girls.It is often played on traditional holidays.

Maqekha

It is a traditional Basotho dance played by Basotho girls .It is often played on moshoeshoe's day. .

sekhankula

It is a traditional Basotho instrument made of a tin and a stick.

Lekope

a mouth-resonated bow,it is a chordophone.

Moropa

Moropa is a Basotho music instrument made a big tin that is rapped by a animal skin.

Khabulu-bulu

a simple instrument created by basotho children for personal amusement.

Boletsi

A cow hair joint together with a stick it is normally used with a Sekhankula instrument.

Lekolulo

a type of a flute played by the herding boys.

Lesiba

the unique stringed-wind instrument consist of a long string attached to a flattened quill and streched to a hardwood stick

Lekhitlane

a wind instumentfasioned from a cow or goat horn.

Thomo

a mouth-resonated musical bow ,it is made of string.

Setolotolo

a braced mouth-resonated bow, often made from bamboo.

Sekebeku sa tsepe

a type of jaw harp,it is made from either wood or metal.

Sekupu

The double-headed basotho drum covered with animal skin.
